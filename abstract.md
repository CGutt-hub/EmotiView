Emotional states are fundamentally embodied, emerging from the dynamic interplay between central neural processing and peripheral physiological adjustments orchestrated by the autonomic nervous system (\gls{ANS}). Understanding how brain activity and these peripheral signals co-vary across emotional conditions is crucial for elucidating brain-body interactions. This thesis reports two studies. The first (EV1, $N = 5$) is a pilot investigation validating a fully automated, modular Nextflow analysis pipeline---the \gls{ATBX}---using simultaneously recorded \gls{EEG}, \gls{ECG}, \gls{EDA}, and \gls{fNIRS} during emotion induction with validated video clips. The second study (EV2, $N = 32$) applies this infrastructure to the publicly available DEAP dataset to test four pre-registered hypotheses about emotional modulation of multimodal physiology at the group level.

EV2 analyses employed Linear Mixed Models (\glspl{LMM}) with condition bin as fixed effect and participant random intercept, separating within-person condition effects from stable between-participant baseline differences. Group-level results confirmed that emotional conditions reliably modulate \gls{EEG} spectral power across frontal and parietal regions (\textbf{\gls{WP}1}): frontal beta showed robust arousal-dependent desynchronisation across all lower-arousal conditions relative to the high-arousal reference (all $p < .001$), while frontal theta was selectively elevated for positive-valence content ($\beta = +73.19$, $p < .001$), consistent with reward-related and approach-motivational engagement of anterior cortical networks. Frontal alpha asymmetry (\gls{FAI}) showed a significant but small group-level effect ($\chi^2 = 12.40$, $p = .030$) dominated by stable between-participant variance (\gls{ICC}~$= .958$), indicating that trait-level hemispheric bias strongly constrains state-dependent modulation. \gls{EDA} and pulse-rate variability \glspl{LMM} produced degenerate model fits, precluding effect-size interpretation from these streams without reprocessing using validated peak detection and phasic/tonic decomposition.

Cross-modal correlation data provided tentative evidence for neural-cardiac co-variation at the trial level ($r = 0.449$ for BVP--\gls{EEG} alpha; \textbf{\gls{WP}2}), though full group-level inference remains a target for the next pipeline cycle (\textbf{\gls{WP}3}). A contextual \gls{LMM} testing whether \gls{FAI} predicts autonomic condition differentiation yielded a marginal, counter-directional within-person slope ($\hat{\beta}_\text{within} = -866$, $p = .059$; \textbf{\gls{WP}4}), inconsistent with the directional prediction of motivational direction theory and attributable in part to a scaling artefact requiring standardisation before reinterpretation.

These results establish a partially confirmed picture of emotional brain-body coordination: \gls{EEG} spectral responses dissociate along arousal and valence dimensions, with frontal beta indexing arousal level and frontal theta selectively marking positive valence. The \gls{ATBX} delivers the methodological infrastructure---modular, Parquet-based, Nextflow-orchestrated, with integrated \gls{LMM} and contextual-\gls{LMM} modules---needed to address the remaining hypotheses in a purpose-built sample with validated autonomic preprocessing.
